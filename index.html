Massive Anti-Piracy Operation in Tamil Nadu

Over 120 individuals arrested for downloading and sharing pirated copy of Ajith's "Good Bad Ugly"

120 Arrested Across Tamil Nadu for Downloading Pirated Version of "Good Bad Ugly"

By Arun Kumar June 15, 2023 5 min read

In a major crackdown on film piracy, Tamil Nadu Cyber Crime Police have arrested 120 individuals across the state for downloading, sharing, or distributing the pirated version of superstar Ajith Kumar's latest blockbuster "Good Bad Ugly". The arrests were made in coordinated raids conducted simultaneously in Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai, Trichy, and Salem.

The movie, which released worldwide today morning, had its HD print leaked online within hours of its theatrical release. The Anti-Piracy Cell of Kollywood immediately alerted authorities, leading to swift action by the Cyber Crime division.

"We have identified and arrested the main culprits who first uploaded the movie to torrent sites and Telegram channels," said Chennai Cyber Crime SP Rajeshwari Natarajan. "Additionally, we've tracked down and arrested users who downloaded the pirated copy through their IP addresses. This sends a strong message that piracy will not be tolerated."

According to police sources, the mastermind behind the leak was traced to a digital studio in Vadapalani, Chennai, where an employee allegedly smuggled out a digital copy during the final quality check. The individual has been detained along with several associates who helped distribute the copy online.

In Madurai, police arrested 28 college students who had formed a WhatsApp group to share the pirated link. "These students knew it was illegal but thought they wouldn't get caught," said Madurai DSP Vijayakumar. "They're now facing serious charges under the Copyright Act and IT Act."

The Tamil Nadu Film Producers Council has welcomed the crackdown. President Vishal Ramaswamy stated, "Piracy is killing our industry. Each illegal download means lost revenue for hundreds of workers. We applaud the police for their swift action."

The police have urged the public to report any pirated links immediately and warned that they are actively monitoring torrent sites and file-sharing platforms. "Enjoy movies in theaters or through legal platforms. Piracy is not a victimless crime," emphasized SP Natarajan.

Meanwhile, "Good Bad Ugly" has opened to record-breaking collections in theaters despite the piracy threat. Fans have taken to social media with #SupportCinema hashtag, encouraging others to watch the film legally.
